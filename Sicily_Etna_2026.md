## 🗺️ THE CONCEPT

**Sicily = Italy's ultimate family island.** Europe's highest active volcano,
ancient Greek temples, crystal-clear beaches, the best street food in Italy,
and guaranteed sunshine. Two bases that give you everything:

**Week 1 (7 nights): Taormina / East Coast** — Mount Etna volcano tours,
Taormina's stunning hilltop town, Alcantara gorge swimming, beaches, boat
trips, and the best views in the Mediterranean.

towns, the most beautiful beaches in Sicily, ancient Greek ruins, incredible
seafood, and a completely different, less-touristy vibe.

## 🌋 THE VOLCANO: MOUNT ETNA

### What is it?
- **Europe's highest active volcano** — 3,327m
- **UNESCO World Heritage Site**
- One of the most active volcanoes in the world — regularly erupts!
- Guided tours take you to ~2,900m with craters, lava flows, steam vents
- **Family-friendly** options available (you don't have to summit)

### Organised Tour Options:

#### OPTION A: Etna Guided Half-Day Tour ⭐ RECOMMENDED FOR FAMILIES
- **Operator:** MountEtnaTours.com, Etna People, Go Etna, or similar
- **Duration:** 5 hours (morning or sunset options)
- **What:** 4x4 transport from base + guided walk on lava fields, craters, caves
- **Level:** Moderate — Tom (8) can manage with encouragement
- **Price:** ~€89pp (children often discounted)
- **Sunset tour** is magical — watching the sun set from 2,500m over the Ionian Sea
- **Book:** mountetnatours.com or viator.com

#### OPTION B: Etna Cable Car + Guide (summit area)
- Cable car from Rifugio Sapienza (1,900m) to 2,500m
- Then 4x4 to ~2,900m + guided walk to crater area
- **Cable car:** ~€35pp each way + guide ~€30pp
- More independent but more expensive overall
- Katie & George would love the adrenaline of being near the summit

#### OPTION C: Etna by Circumetnea Railway 🚂
- Historic narrow-gauge railway that circles the base of Etna
- Catania Borgo → Riposto, ~3hrs
- **Not a summit experience** but a lovely scenic train ride through lava fields and vineyards
- Tom would love the train! Could combine with a half-day hike
- Very cheap — regular train fares

### What to bring:
- Closed shoes (essential — lava rock is sharp)
- Windproof jacket (even in August — it's cold at altitude!)
- Sun protection + water
- Camera!

## 🏖️ WEEK 1: TAORMINA / EAST COAST (7 nights)

### Where to stay:

#### OPTION A: Holiday apartment/villa (Airbnb/Booking.com) ⭐
- **Area:** Giardini Naxos (beach town below Taormina) or Letojanni
- 2-3 bed apartment or small villa with pool
- **Price estimate:** €100-200/night = **£600-1,200 for 7 nights**
- **Giardini Naxos** = beachside, lively, restaurants, supermarkets
- **Letojanni** = quieter beach town, good for families

#### OPTION B: Campsite — Camping San Leo (Letojanni)
- Near the beach, close to Taormina
- Basic but affordable, pitches + some mobile homes
- **Price estimate:** €50-80/night = **£300-500 for 7 nights**

#### OPTION C: Hotel/resort
- Family hotels in Giardini Naxos with pools
- **Price estimate:** €120-250/night = **£750-1,500 for 7 nights**

### 🎯 Things to do — Week 1:

#### 🌋 MOUNT ETNA GUIDED TOUR ⭐⭐⭐ THE HIGHLIGHT
- Book a half-day or sunset tour (see volcano section above)
- **This is the big one** — walking on an active volcano!
- George will be mind-blown. Katie will get the Instagram shots. Tom will tell the story for years.

#### 🏛️ TAORMINA
- One of Italy's most dramatically beautiful hilltop towns
- **Ancient Greek Theatre** — 2,300 years old, with Etna as its backdrop
- Corso Umberto — main street, shopping, gelato, cafés
- Stunning views from every angle
- Evening visit recommended (cooler, lit up, amazing atmosphere)
- Cable car down to Isola Bella beach

#### 🏖️ ISOLA BELLA
- Tiny island connected to shore by a sand strip
- Crystal-clear swimming, snorkelling
- One of Sicily's most iconic beaches
- Cable car from Taormina down to the beach (Tom will love!)

#### 🌊 ALCANTARA GORGE ⭐ TEENS WILL LOVE THIS
- Dramatic volcanic basalt canyon with freezing river water rushing through
- Wade/swim through the gorge — water is COLD but thrilling!
- Tom can paddle at the entrance, Katie & George can go deeper
- Body rafting and canyoning tours available (min age usually 10-12)
- **30 min drive from Taormina**
- Entry ~€13 + lift, or free to walk down

#### 🏙️ CATANIA (45 min drive)
- Sicily's second city — vibrant, chaotic, real
- **La Pescheria fish market** — one of Europe's most famous markets. Incredible experience.
- Piazza del Duomo — elephant fountain, cathedral
- Via Etnea — main shopping street with Etna views
- **Street food capital:** arancini, cipollina, cartocciate. Cheap and amazing.
- Evening: buzzing nightlife around Piazza Teatro Massimo

#### 🏖️ BEACHES AROUND TAORMINA
- **Isola Bella** — iconic, turquoise, snorkelling
- **Letojanni Beach** — long sandy/pebble beach, less crowded
- **Giardini Naxos Beach** — sandy, all facilities, waterfront restaurants
- **Mazzarò Beach** — pebble, crystal clear, cable car access

#### 🚤 BOAT TRIP
- Hire a small boat or book a tour from Giardini Naxos
- Explore hidden coves, Grotta Azzurra, swim in turquoise water
- Half-day trips ~€30-50pp
- Some include snorkelling stops and aperitivo

## 🏖️ WEEK 2: SOUTH-EAST SICILY (7 nights)

### Getting there:
Drive Taormina → Syracuse: 1.5hrs on the autostrada. Easy.

### Where to stay:

#### OPTION A: Apartment/villa near the coast ⭐
- **Area:** Fontane Bianche (8km south of Syracuse) — famous sandy beach
- Or Avola/Noto Marina — beautiful beaches, close to Noto
- 2-3 bed apartment or villa with pool
- **Price estimate:** €80-160/night = **£500-950 for 7 nights**

#### OPTION B: Agriturismo (farm stay)
- Stay on a working farm with pool, home-cooked Sicilian food
- Amazing experience for kids — animals, olive groves, citrus orchards
- **Price estimate:** €90-180/night = **£550-1,100 for 7 nights** (often includes dinner!)

### 🎯 Things to do — Week 2:

#### 🏛️ SYRACUSE (SIRACUSA) ⭐⭐⭐
- One of the most important cities of the ancient world
- **Ortigia Island** — the old town, completely surrounded by sea
  - Wander narrow baroque streets, eat at waterfront restaurants
  - Temple of Apollo, Piazza Duomo (cathedral built INTO a Greek temple!)
  - Fonte Aretusa — freshwater spring with papyrus plants, right by the sea
- **Neapolis Archaeological Park**
  - Greek Theatre (still hosts performances in summer!)
  - Ear of Dionysius — enormous cave with incredible acoustics
  - Roman Amphitheatre
- Entry ~€16 adults, children discounted
- **Evening in Ortigia** is absolute magic — dinner by the sea, gelato, buskers

#### 🏖️ BEACHES — THE BEST IN SICILY
- **Fontane Bianche** — white sand, turquoise water, shallow. Tom's paradise.
- **San Lorenzo (Marzamemi)** — stunning wild beach, crystal clear
- **Calamosche** — hidden cove inside a nature reserve. Short walk to access. Breathtaking.
- **Isola delle Correnti** — Sicily's southernmost point, where two seas meet
- **Vendicari Nature Reserve** — flamingos + pristine beaches

#### 🏛️ NOTO ⭐ UNESCO BAROQUE MASTERPIECE
- Called "the most beautiful baroque town in Europe"
- Every building is golden limestone, rebuilt after a 1693 earthquake
- Corso Vittorio Emanuele — the main street is jaw-droppingly beautiful
- **Caffè Sicilia** — famous for the best granita and cannoli in Sicily
- Evening visit with golden hour light is unforgettable

#### 🏛️ RAGUSA IBLA (1hr drive)
- Another UNESCO baroque town, perched on a hilltop
- Quieter than Noto, incredibly atmospheric
- The "other Ragusa" (the town has two halves connected by stairs)
- Filming location for Inspector Montalbano (George may know it)
- Amazing restaurants

#### 🐟 MARZAMEMI
- Tiny fishing village, photogenic piazza right on the sea
- One of Sicily's most charming villages
- Excellent seafood restaurants
- Tuna history — old tonnara (tuna fishery)
- Great for a half-day visit + lunch

#### 🏛️ MODICA (1hr drive)
- ANOTHER UNESCO baroque gem — they're everywhere down here!
- Famous for **Modica chocolate** — ancient Aztec-style chocolate, grainy and unique
- Katie will love the chocolate shops. George will eat his weight in it.

## 🗒️ PRACTICAL NOTES

- **Etna in August:** Even in summer it's cold at altitude. Bring jackets and closed shoes!
- **Etna safety:** Always go with a licensed guide for the summit area. The volcano is actively monitored.
- **Driving in Sicily:** Roads are fine, but Sicilians drive confidently. Catania traffic is chaotic — park on outskirts.
- **Parking in Taormina:** Don't drive into the town — use the car parks below and walk or take the cable car up.
- **Syracuse Ortigia:** Park outside the island (Parcheggio Talete) and walk in.
- **August in Sicily:** Hot! 35-40°C is normal. Do sightseeing mornings/evenings. Beach/pool in the afternoon.
- **Ferragosto (15 Aug):** Italy's biggest holiday. You're leaving on the 12th so you miss the worst of it, but things start getting busier from the 10th.
- **Currency:** Euro. English is spoken in tourist areas but less than Croatia. Learn basic Italian — locals love it.
- **Water:** Safe to drink from taps but many locals prefer bottled.
- **Alcantara gorge water:** Fed by Etna snowmelt. FREEZING COLD even in August. That's half the fun!
