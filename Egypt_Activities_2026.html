🔥 Extremely hot and bone dry. Zero rain. 13 hours of sunshine daily. Hurghada's Red Sea coast is slightly more bearable than Cairo/Luxor thanks to coastal breezes, but midday is brutal (35°C+). Snorkelling and diving are perfect — sea is 28-29°C with excellent visibility. Cairo/Luxor day trips will be sweltering (40°C+) — start very early. Humidity is surprisingly low (40-55%).

Cultural & Practical

4 activities
47

The Art of Haggling

Haggling is expected and enjoyed everywhere in Egypt — markets, taxis, boat trips, souvenirs. Start at 50% of the asking price and work from there. Get the kids involved — they'll love it! A genuine life skill and a fun game.
✅ Tom ⭐⭐⭐⭐ ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Teens 🆓 Free (saves you money!) 🧭 Practical
48

Egyptian Spice Shopping

Buy incredible spices at a fraction of UK prices — saffron, cumin, hibiscus tea, dried mango. Khan El-Khalili and Luxor souks have the best selection. The colours and smells are intoxicating. Great gifts for grandparents!
✅ Tom ⭐⭐⭐ ✅ Teens £2-10 < £25pp 🧭 Practical
49

Papyrus & Cartouche Souvenir

Get your name written in hieroglyphics on a cartouche pendant or papyrus painting. Sold everywhere near the temples and bazaars. Tom can get his name in the same script the pharaohs used. Unique keepsake.
✅ Tom ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ ✅ Teens £3-15 < £25pp 🧭 Practical
50

E-Visa & Arrival Experience

UK citizens need a tourist visa — buy e-visa online at visa2egypt.gov.eg (~£20pp) at least 7 days before travel. Or buy visa-on-arrival (~$25pp). Currency is Egyptian Pound (£1 ≈ 60 EGP). Everything is incredibly cheap!
✅ Tom ✅ Teens ~£20pp < £25pp 🧭 Practical
