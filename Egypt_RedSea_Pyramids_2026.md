## 🏛️ OVERNIGHT 1: LUXOR — VALLEY OF THE KINGS (Days 6-7)

### Getting there:
- **Option A: Domestic flight** — Hurghada → Luxor, 45 min, ~£40-60pp return. Fast!
- **Option B: Private car/minibus transfer** — 3-3.5hrs drive through the Eastern Desert. Scenic but long.
- **Option C: Organised overnight tour** — most convenient, everything arranged. ~£80-150pp including transport, guide, accommodation, meals. Book via hotel or viator.com/GetYourGuide.

### What you'll see:

#### 🏛️ VALLEY OF THE KINGS ⭐⭐⭐ BUCKET LIST
- Where the pharaohs were buried — Tutankhamun's tomb is HERE
- Walk into actual royal tombs carved deep into rock, covered in 3,300-year-old paintings
- 3 tombs included in standard ticket (~£15pp). Tutankhamun's tomb extra (~£25pp) — **worth it**
- **"We stood inside the tomb of an Egyptian pharaoh" = life story material**
- Tom will be buzzing. George will be in awe. Katie will get the photos.
- Go early morning (before 9am) — it gets extremely hot

#### 🏛️ TEMPLE OF HATSHEPSUT
- Dramatic three-tiered temple carved into a cliff face
- Built for Egypt's most powerful female pharaoh
- Incredible architecture against the desert mountains

#### 🏛️ KARNAK TEMPLE ⭐⭐⭐
- The largest ancient religious site in the world
- Forest of massive columns — 134 columns, some 21m high
- Walking through it feels like being in an Indiana Jones film
- Sound and light show in the evening (if staying overnight)

#### 🏛️ LUXOR TEMPLE
- Right in the centre of modern Luxor, lit up beautifully at night
- Avenue of Sphinxes connecting to Karnak (recently restored!)
- Visit at sunset/evening for the most dramatic experience

#### 🏛️ COLOSSI OF MEMNON
- Two massive 18m stone statues guarding the Nile floodplain
- Free to visit, on the way to Valley of the Kings

### Where to stay in Luxor (1 night):
- **Steigenberger Nile Palace** — on the Nile, pool, family rooms, ~£60-100/night
- **Sonesta St. George** — Nile views, pool, good value
- **Budget:** Simple clean hotel ~£30-50/night
- Or included if you book an organised overnight tour

### Estimated cost for Luxor overnight (5 pax):
- Organised tour: ~£400-750 (transport, guide, hotels, entries, meals)
- Self-organised: flights ~£200-300 + hotel ~£60-100 + entries ~£100 + guide ~£80-120 = £440-620

## 🏛️ OVERNIGHT 2: CAIRO & PYRAMIDS (Days 13-14)

### Getting there:
- **Domestic flight:** Hurghada → Cairo, ~1hr, ~£50-80pp return
- **Organised tour:** ~£100-180pp all-inclusive (transport, guide, hotel, entries)
- **Drive:** 5-6hrs — not recommended with kids

### What you'll see:

#### 🏛️ THE GREAT PYRAMIDS OF GIZA ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ THE #1 BUCKET LIST ITEM
- The last surviving Wonder of the Ancient World
- 4,500 years old and still standing
- **You can GO INSIDE the Great Pyramid** (narrow passage to burial chamber — claustrophobic but incredible. Extra ~£15pp)
- Photo at the panorama viewpoint with all 3 pyramids
- **THE single most impressive thing any of you will ever see**

#### 🏛️ THE GREAT SPHINX
- Half-lion, half-human, carved from a single piece of limestone
- Right next to the pyramids
- Tom will have seen this in school — now he's standing in front of it

#### 🐪 CAMEL RIDE AT THE PYRAMIDS ⭐
- Ride a camel around the pyramids with the Sahara behind you
- ~£10-20pp for a short ride
- Photo opportunity of a lifetime
- **Tom on a camel in front of the Pyramids = framed forever**

#### 🏛️ EGYPTIAN MUSEUM (or Grand Egyptian Museum)
- **Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)** — brand new, near the Pyramids, THE place to see Tutankhamun's treasures
- Gold death mask, chariots, jewellery, sarcophagi
- After seeing his tomb in Luxor, now you see his treasure in Cairo
- Entry ~£20pp

#### 🏛️ KHAN EL-KHALILI BAZAAR
- Cairo's ancient souk — hundreds of years old
- Spices, lanterns, jewellery, scarves, perfumes
- **Katie will love shopping here. George will love the chaos. Tom will love the smells.**
- Haggling is expected and fun — teach the kids!
- Stop for mint tea at El Fishawy café (allegedly the oldest café in Cairo)

### Where to stay in Cairo (1 night):
- **Marriott Mena House** — literally at the foot of the Pyramids. Wake up to pyramid views! Premium but unforgettable. ~£120-200/night.
- **Steigenberger Hotel El Tahrir** — central, pool, good value. ~£50-80/night.
- Or included in organised tour package.

### Estimated cost for Cairo overnight (5 pax):
- Organised tour: ~£500-900 (flights, guide, hotel, entries, meals, camel ride)
- Self-organised: flights ~£250-400 + hotel ~£80-200 + entries ~£100 + guide ~£80-120 + camels ~£50-100 = £560-920

## 🗒️ PRACTICAL NOTES

- **Visa:** UK citizens need a tourist visa. Buy **e-visa in advance** at visa2egypt.gov.eg (~£20pp) or buy visa-on-arrival (~$25pp). Apply at least 7 days before travel.
- **Currency:** Egyptian Pound (EGP). £1 ≈ ~60 EGP. Everything is incredibly cheap by UK standards. Take some USD or EUR — widely accepted for tours/tips.
- **August heat:** Hurghada 35-40°C. Luxor/Cairo 40-45°C! Drink constantly, wear sunscreen, sightsee early morning. The sea and pools are your friend.
- **Luxor heat warning:** Valley of the Kings can hit 45°C. Go at opening (6am). Bring water, hat, sunscreen. Worth it, but be prepared.
- **All-inclusive tips:** Check what's included — some AIs include premium drinks, some don't. Water sports and excursions are usually extra.
- **Tipping culture:** Tipping is expected everywhere in Egypt. Budget £5-10/day for small tips (toilet attendants, luggage porters, boat crew, etc.). Have small notes ready.
- **Safety:** Hurghada resort areas are very safe and tourist-friendly. Luxor and Cairo tourist sites are well-policed. Normal tourist precautions apply.
- **Haggling:** Expected and enjoyed! Markets, taxis, boat trips — always negotiate. Get the kids involved, they'll love it.
- **Stomach care:** Drink only bottled water (provided at resort). Avoid salad/raw veg outside the resort. Resort food is safe.
- **Photography:** You can photograph almost everything, but some tombs charge extra for camera use (~£5). Guides will tell you.
- **Red Sea snorkelling:** Wear reef shoes — sea urchins exist. Don't touch coral (it's protected and also sharp!).
- **This is NOT a Europe trip.** It's noisier, hotter, more chaotic, and infinitely more exciting. Embrace it.
