## 🚢 FERRY/CATAMARAN CONNECTIONS

All catamarans are **passenger-only** (no cars). Three operators run the Dubrovnik→Split island-hopping route in summer:

### Operators:
1. **Krilo (Kapetan Luka)** - krilostar.com - Main operator, most departures
2. **TP Line** - tp-line.hr - Additional sailings
3. **Jadrolinija** - jadrolinija.hr - State ferry company

### Key Routes:

| Leg | Distance | Duration | Approx Cost (5 pax) | Typical Departure |
|-----|----------|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Dubrovnik → Korčula | 110km | ~2 hrs | £80-120 | Morning (usually 08:00-10:00) |
| Korčula → Hvar | 61km | 1-1.5 hrs | £60-90 | Morning (usually 08:00-09:00) |
| Hvar → Split | 37km | 1-1.25 hrs | £50-80 | Multiple daily |
| **Total ferries estimate** | | | **£190-290** | |

### ⚠️ LUGGAGE ON CATAMARANS
- **20kg per person** = 100kg total for your family
- Small cabin bags also allowed
- Luggage stored in hold/deck area
- **TIP:** Pack into bags not hard suitcases — easier to stow on ferries
- **BOOK IN ADVANCE** for July/August — catamarans sell out in peak season!

### 📋 Booking:
- **Bookaway.com** — easiest single site to compare all operators and book
- **krilostar.com** — book Krilo direct (sometimes cheaper)
- **jadrolinija.hr** — book Jadrolinija direct

## 🏨 ACCOMMODATION DETAILS

### 1. DUBROVNIK — 3 nights (29 Jul – 2 Aug)

**What to look for:** Apartment near Gruž Port (where ferries depart) OR near Old Town. For 5 people you need a 2-bedroom apartment minimum.

#### Recommended search (Airbnb/Booking.com):
- **Search:** Dubrovnik, 29 Jul – 2 Aug, 2 adults + 3 children
- **Area:** Lapad or Gruž (close to ferry port, beaches, supermarkets — more affordable than Old Town)
- **Budget:** £100-180/night for a 2-bed apartment = £300-540 total

#### Specific places to look for:
- **Apartments Minerva, Lapad** — Family apartments, pool, 10 min walk to Lapad beach
- **Villa Franica, Lapad** — 2-bed apartments with sea views
- **Apartments near Gruž harbour** — Convenient for ferry departure day

#### What to do in Dubrovnik:
- Walk the City Walls (amazing but hot — go early morning)
- Cable car up Mount Srđ
- Kayak around the walls
- Day trip to Lokrum Island (15 min ferry from Old Port)
- Banje Beach / Lapad Beach (more family-friendly)
- Game of Thrones tour (Katie & George may enjoy!)

---

### 2. KORČULA — 2 nights (2 Aug – 4 Aug)

**Getting there:** Morning catamaran from Dubrovnik, arriving ~10:00-11:00. Ferry arrives right at Old Town harbour — walk to accommodation.

#### Recommended search:
- **Search:** Korčula Town, 2-4 Aug, 2 adults + 3 children
- **Area:** Korčula Old Town or just outside the walls
- **Budget:** £80-140/night = £160-280 total

#### Specific places to look for:
- **Apartments Lešić Dimitri** — In the Old Town, upmarket but stunning
- **Hotel Korčula De La Ville** — Right on the harbour waterfront
- **Apartments near Banje Beach** — Short walk from town, good for families
- **Villa Korčula** — Family-run apartments with sea views

#### What to do on Korčula:
- Explore the Old Town (mini Dubrovnik, much less crowded!)
- Swim at Pupnatska Luka or Vela Pržina (sandy!) beaches
- Moreška sword dance (performed weekly in summer — Tom will love this)
- Marco Polo Museum (he's allegedly from Korčula)
- Rent bikes and explore the island
- Wine tasting — Pošip white wine is local specialty

---

### 3. HVAR — 3 nights (4 Aug – 7 Aug)

**Getting there:** Morning catamaran from Korčula to Hvar Town (~1-1.5hrs). Ferry docks at Hvar Town harbour.

#### Recommended search:
- **Search:** Hvar Town, 4-7 Aug, 2 adults + 3 children
- **Area:** Hvar Town or Stari Grad (quieter, more family-oriented)
- **Budget:** £100-180/night = £300-540 total
- ⚠️ Hvar is the most expensive island — book early!

#### Specific places to look for:
- **Heritage Hotel Nava, Hvar Town** — Central, family rooms available
- **Hotel Pharia, Hvar Town** — Mid-range, good location
- **Apartments near Hvar harbour** — Best value for families
- **Villa Nora, Hvar** — Family apartments with pool, outside town

**Alternative: Stay in Stari Grad instead**
- Quieter, cheaper, more family-friendly than Hvar Town
- Beautiful UNESCO-listed Stari Grad Plain
- **Apartments Stari Grad** — various family options on Booking.com
- Note: Stari Grad is where car ferries dock (separate from catamarans)

#### What to do on Hvar:
- Climb to Fortica Fortress (panoramic views of Pakleni Islands)
- Boat trip to Pakleni Islands (swimming, snorkelling, Blue Lagoon)
- Dubovica Beach (gorgeous cove, 20 min drive — taxi/bus needed)
- Stari Grad and the UNESCO Plain
- Lavender fields and shops (Hvar = "Lavender Island")
- Hvar Town waterfront promenade — great for evening stroll & dinner

### 4. SPLIT AREA — 5 nights (7 Aug – 12 Aug)

**Getting there:** Catamaran from Hvar to Split (~1hr). Arrives at Split Ferry Port.

For the Split leg, you have two great options:

#### OPTION A: Campsite — Stobreč Split Camping ⭐ RECOMMENDED
- **Website:** camping.hr/campsites/stobrec-split
- **Location:** 7km from Split centre, on the coast
- **Mobile homes:** Available for 6 people (2-3 bedrooms, kitchen, bathroom, AC, terrace) — 40m²
- **Facilities:** 2 pebble beaches (Blue Flag), 3 pools (inc. kids pool), waterfall features, pool bar, wellness/spa, inflatable water park, restaurant, supermarket
- **Activities:** Kayaking, SUP, diving lessons, cageball, basketball, volleyball, kids club, SUP yoga
- **Bus to Split centre:** Regular service from nearby stop
- **Price estimate:** €80-150/night for a 6-person mobile home in peak season = £350-650 total
- **Book direct** at camping.hr or via booking platforms

#### OPTION B: Campsite — Almissa Camping (Omiš)
- **Website:** camping.hr/campsites/almissa
- **Location:** Omiš, 21km SE of Split, at mouth of River Cetina
- **Mobile homes:** 6+2 person homes (40m²), some with hot tub on terrace
- **Facilities:** Sandy beach (great for Tom!), pools, restaurant
- **Unique selling point:** ADVENTURE ACTIVITIES — rafting on Cetina river, ziplining, canyoning, quad biking
- **Price estimate:** €70-130/night = £300-560 total
- **⚡ Best for:** Adrenaline-seeking families — Katie & George would love the rafting/ziplining!

#### OPTION C: Airbnb Apartment in Split
- **Search:** Split, 7-12 Aug, 2 adults + 3 children
- **Area:** Bačvice (near beach), Firule, or Žnjan — all near beaches and walkable to Old Town
- **Budget:** £100-160/night = £500-800 total

#### 🚗 HIRE CAR FOR SPLIT LEG (Recommended)
Since you're staying 5 nights and may want to explore, consider hiring a car for this leg only:
- Pick up at Split Ferry Port on arrival (7 Aug)
- Drop off at Split Airport before flight (13 Aug morning)
- Gives flexibility for day trips and getting to/from campsite
- **Estimate:** £200-350 for 6 days
- **Book via:** rentalcars.com or discovercars.com
- **Note:** You need a car anyway to get from Stobreč/Omiš campsite to the airport on departure day

#### What to do around Split:
- Diocletian's Palace (UNESCO) — explore the 1,700-year-old Roman ruins
- Climb the Cathedral of St Domnius bell tower
- Marjan Hill hike — panoramic views over Split and islands
- Bačvice Beach (sandy, close to Old Town)
- **Day trip: Krka National Park** — stunning waterfalls, you can swim! (1hr drive)
- **Day trip: Trogir** — beautiful UNESCO island town (30 min drive)
- **Day trip: Brač island** — famous Zlatni Rat (Golden Horn) beach (1hr ferry from Split)
- Riva promenade evening stroll — buzzing with cafes and atmosphere

## 🗒️ PRACTICAL TIPS

- **Currency:** Euro (Croatia adopted € in January 2023)
- **Visas:** None needed for UK citizens
- **Water:** Tap water is safe to drink everywhere
- **SIM cards:** Buy at Split or Dubrovnik airports (T-Mobile, A1, or Tele2)
- **Dubrovnik ferry port:** Gruž (NOT Old Town) — ~3km from Old Town
- **Luggage strategy:** Use soft bags/holdalls rather than hard suitcases for catamaran travel
- **Peak season tip:** Dubrovnik Old Town and Hvar Town get very crowded in August — visit early morning or evening
- **Travel insurance:** Essential — covers ferry cancellations due to weather
- **Neum corridor:** If anyone drives Dubrovnik→Split by road, the route passes through a short stretch of Bosnia (Neum). No visa needed, but take passports. There's also the new Pelješac Bridge bypassing Neum entirely.
