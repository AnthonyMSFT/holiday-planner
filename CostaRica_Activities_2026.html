🇨🇷 The Holiday at a Glance

Pure tropical adventure — zip-lining over cloud forests, soaking in volcanic hot springs, white-water rafting through jungle canyons, and spotting sloths, monkeys, and toucans around every corner. Start in Arenal for the volcano and hot springs, cross to Monteverde's misty cloud forest for hanging bridges and night wildlife walks, then hit the Pacific coast for surf lessons, catamaran cruises, and bioluminescent kayaking.

Costa Rica is the most adventurous destination on the list. George will want to do every zip line and rafting trip going. Tom will be obsessed with the sloths and the Jaguar Rescue Centre. Katie will photograph the blue Río Celeste waterfall and the sunset from a catamaran. Night tours through the cloud forest — spotting glowing frogs and kinkajous by torchlight — are genuinely magical. "Pura Vida" isn't just a saying here, it's a way of life.

"Green Season"

🌦️ August is rainy season — but don't panic! Mornings are typically sunny and dry. Heavy downpours hit in the afternoon/evening and clear quickly. The Pacific coast (Guanacaste, Manuel Antonio) gets the most rain. The Caribbean side can actually be drier in August. Arenal is cloudy and humid. The upside? It's "green season" — lush rainforests, fewer tourists, lower prices, and baby turtle season! Pack a lightweight rain jacket and quick-dry clothes. Plan outdoor adventures for morning.

🌧️ Rainy Season — What It's Actually Like

August is "green season" — but don't panic. Here's the reality:

☀️ Mornings (7am–1pm)
Almost always sunny and dry. This is when you do all outdoor activities — hikes, zip lines, rafting, beach, waterfall trips.
🌧️ Afternoons (1–4pm)
Heavy tropical downpour — dramatic but usually only 1-2 hours. Not drizzle — proper rain. Then it clears.
🌅 Evenings (5pm+)
Rain usually stops. Sunsets can be spectacular. Perfect for dinner, sunset cruises, or hot springs.
Does anything close?
🏨 Hotels — All open, covered pools & spa
🎯 Tours — Run rain or shine (guides are used to it)
🏪 Shops — Open as normal
🌿 National Parks — Open as normal
🍽️ Restaurants — All open
♨️ Hot springs — Even BETTER in the rain!

The rhythm: Adventure mornings → chill afternoons (hot springs, chocolate tours, spa, cooking classes) → clear evenings. The rain makes rafting better (higher rivers), wildlife more active (frogs!), and hot springs more atmospheric. Pack a lightweight rain jacket and quick-dry clothes. The upside? Fewer tourists, lower prices, lush green landscapes, and baby turtle nesting season. 🐢
