🇯🇵 The Holiday at a Glance

The ultimate bucket-list family adventure — bullet trains between neon-lit Tokyo, ancient Kyoto, and foodie Osaka. Tokyo delivers Disneyland, DisneySea, teamLab's mind-bending digital art, Akihabara's gaming arcades, and Harajuku's wild fashion. Kyoto is a different world: bamboo forests, monkey parks, ninja experiences, and thousands of vermillion torii gates. Osaka brings Universal Studios, Super Nintendo World, and the best street food in Japan.

George will lose his mind in Super Nintendo World and Akihabara's multi-floor arcades. Katie will fill her phone at teamLab and Harajuku. Tom will be wide-eyed feeding deer in Nara and riding the Shinkansen at 320km/h. And the food — from conveyor-belt sushi to ramen streets to making your own Cup Noodle — is an adventure in itself. Japan is intense, beautiful, and utterly unforgettable.

🥵 Hot, humid, and unpredictable. August in Japan is VERY hot and sticky (75-80% humidity). Rain is common and typhoons are a real risk — monitor forecasts and have indoor backup plans. That said, this is peak festival (matsuri) season! Carry a small towel, stay hydrated, and plan outdoor activities for morning/evening. Air-conditioned trains, malls, and museums are your midday friends. Kyoto is the hottest city.
