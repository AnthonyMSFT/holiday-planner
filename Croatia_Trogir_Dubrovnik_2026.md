### Day trips from Trogir:

#### 🏛️ TROGIR OLD TOWN (5km / taxi boat)
- **UNESCO World Heritage Site** — one of the best-preserved Romanesque towns in Europe
- Cathedral of St Lawrence with stunning portal
- Kamerlengo Fortress — climb for views, summer cinema screenings!
- Tiny island town connected by bridges — charming narrow streets
- Excellent restaurants on the waterfront (riva)
- **Taxi boat from campsite/Seget Vranjica every 30 min** — easy evening out
- Free to explore, just wander and eat ice cream

#### 🏛️ SPLIT (30km / 30 min drive)
- **Diocletian's Palace** — 1,700-year-old Roman palace, UNESCO, still inhabited
- Cathedral bell tower — climb for panoramic views
- Marjan Hill hike
- Bačvice Beach — sandy, great for teens
- Riva promenade evening stroll
- Fish market (Peškarija) — atmospheric

#### 🌿 KRKA NATIONAL PARK (1hr drive)
- Stunning waterfalls you can swim beneath
- Boardwalk trails through cascading pools
- Visovac Island monastery (boat trip)
- Entry ~€15 adults, children discounted
- Arrive before 10am in August

#### 🏝️ ŠOLTA ISLAND (ferry from Trogir/Split)
- Quiet island with beautiful coves, olive groves, honey production
- Easy half-day or full-day trip by ferry
- Less touristy than Brač or Hvar — peaceful family day

#### ⚡ CETINA RIVER RAFTING (Omiš, 50 min drive)
- Class II-III rapids through limestone canyon
- 3hrs guided, all equipment provided, from ~€35-50pp
- Tom (8) can join on family-friendly sections
- Combine with Omiš pirate fortress visit

## 🏛️ WEEK 2: DUBROVNIK (6 nights)

### Getting there:
**Drive Day (Day 8, Tue 5 Aug):** Trogir → Dubrovnik
- Distance: ~230km
- Time: 3-3.5hrs via A1 motorway + coast road
- Route passes through **Neum** (Bosnia, 9km stretch) — no visa needed, take passports
- OR take the new **Pelješac Bridge** to avoid Bosnia entirely
- **Suggestion:** Stop in **Omiš** for lunch and a walk (pirate town, 30 min from Trogir), then continue south
- Or stop at **Makarska** for a swim — beautiful Riviera beaches

### Where to stay in Dubrovnik:

#### OPTION A: Airbnb Apartment ⭐ RECOMMENDED
- **Area:** Lapad or Gruž (near beaches, shops, bus to Old Town)

#### OPTION C: Camp Solitudo, Dubrovnik

- Mobile homes and pitches, beach, pool
- **Price estimate:** €70-120/night = **£300-500 for 6 nights**
- **Website:** campingsolitudo.com

### 🎯 Things to do in Dubrovnik:

#### MUST-DO for EVERYONE:
- **Walk the City Walls** ⭐ — 2km circuit around the old town, stunning views (go early, 8am opening, before the heat!)
- **Cable car to Mount Srđ** — panoramic views over the old town, islands, and coast
- **Lokrum Island** — 15 min ferry from Old Port. Botanical gardens, peacocks, swimming, Game of Thrones Iron Throne, Dead Sea lake. Perfect family half-day.
- **Banje Beach** — the famous beach right below the old town walls
- **Stradun** — main street of the Old Town, marble-polished, buzzing evening atmosphere

#### For Katie (17) & George (15):
- **Kayaking around the City Walls** ⭐ — paddle right under the fortress walls, stop for swimming at a cave beach
- **Game of Thrones locations** — walk the filming spots (King's Landing, Red Keep, etc.)
- **Cliff bar (Buža)** — famous bar built into the cliff walls outside the Old Town. Swim off the rocks, drink in hand. Incredible.
- **SUP** on Lapad Bay
- **Snorkelling** at Lokrum
- **Old Town evening atmosphere** — Stradun, ice cream, live music

#### For Tom (8):
- **Lokrum Island** — peacocks everywhere, swimming in Dead Sea saltwater lake, fortress to explore
- **Cable car** — he'll love the ride up and the views
- **Beaches** — Banje, Lapad, Copacabana (all have shallow areas)
- **City Walls** — it's like walking on a castle (warn: hot and long, bring water/snacks)
- **Fort Lovrijenac** — real fortress overlooking the sea, feels like a pirate castle

#### For Adults:
- **Old Town evening stroll** — Stradun promenade, wine bars, rooftop restaurants
- **Wine tasting** — Pelješac peninsula day trip (Dingač wines)
- **Cavtat** — charming nearby coastal town, 30 min drive, lovely for lunch
- **Dubrovnik's markets and restaurants** — fresh seafood, Dalmatian cuisine

### Day trips from Dubrovnik:

#### 🇲🇪 MONTENEGRO DAY TRIP (1.5hr drive) ⭐
- **Kotor** — UNESCO fjord town, city walls climb, stunning bay
- **Perast** — tiny baroque town, boat to Our Lady of the Rocks island
- **Budva** — old town + beaches
- Cross the border (UK passport, no visa needed)
- One of the most dramatic day trips in Europe
- **Drive or book a tour** (~€40-60pp for guided tour)

#### 🏝️ ELAPHITI ISLANDS
- Three-island boat tour (Koločep, Lopud, Šipan)
- Swimming stops, lunch included
- Lopud has a sandy beach (Šunj) — rare in this area!
- Full day, ~€40-60pp

#### 🏛️ MOSTAR, BOSNIA (2.5hr drive)
- Famous Stari Most (Old Bridge) — watch divers jump from it
- Ottoman old town, coppersmith bazaar
- Completely different culture and architecture
- No visa needed

## 🗒️ PRACTICAL NOTES

- **Amadria Park Trogir → Split Airport:** Only 13km! Closest campsite to any Croatian airport.
- **Taxi boat to Trogir:** Runs every 30 min from nearby Seget Vranjica. Magic for evening dinners.
- **Pelješac Bridge:** Opened 2022, bypasses the Neum (Bosnia) corridor. Use this on the drive south to avoid border hassle.
- **Dubrovnik Old Town parking:** Don't try to park in Old Town. Stay in Lapad/Gruž and take bus #6 (runs every 15 min, ~€2pp).
- **City Walls tip:** Open 8am in summer. Go at opening — it gets brutally hot and crowded by 10am. Bring water and hats.
- **Dubrovnik Card:** Available for 1/3/7 days. Includes City Walls, museums, bus travel. Worth it for 6 nights.
- **Lokrum ferry:** Runs every 30 min from Old Port, ~€15 return. No overnight stays allowed on the island (it's a nature reserve).
- **Montenegro border:** UK passport holders — no visa. Can be queues in August (30-60 min). Go early.
- **Dubrovnik in August:** Very busy. Old Town is best early morning or after 6pm.
- **Currency:** Euro in Croatia. Euro also accepted widely in Montenegro (even though their official currency is also Euro now).
