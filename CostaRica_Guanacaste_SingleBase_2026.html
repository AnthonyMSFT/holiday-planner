🇨🇷 The Holiday at a Glance

The smart choice for August — Guanacaste is Costa Rica’s driest region, even in rainy season. Base yourselves at an all-inclusive resort on the Papagayo coast, and you’ve got golden beaches, world-class surfing, volcanic mud baths, zip-line canopy tours, and snorkelling trips all within an hour. Day trips to Rincón de la Vieja volcano bring hot springs, waterfall hikes, and river tubing. And you’re just 30 minutes from Liberia airport — no 3-hour cross-country transfers.

George will be first in line for the Guachipelín adventure park. Tom will go wild on the resort water slides and the estuary crocodile boat tour. Katie will photograph Playa Conchal’s white shell beach and the sunset from a catamaran. On rest days, you’re at an all-inclusive with pools, beach, and unlimited food.

☀️ Guanacaste is Costa Rica’s DRIEST region. While the rest of the country gets heavy afternoon rain in August, Guanacaste sees significantly less rainfall. Mornings are hot and sunny, with brief afternoon showers that clear quickly. Sea temperature is perfect for swimming. UV is extreme — factor 50 essential. The all-inclusive resort model works brilliantly here: beach in the morning, pool when it rains, sunset cocktails when it clears.

🌧️ Rainy Season — What It's Actually Like

August is "green season" — but don't panic. Here's the reality:

☀️ Mornings (7am–1pm)
Almost always sunny and dry. This is when you do all outdoor activities — hikes, zip lines, rafting, beach, waterfall trips.
🌧️ Afternoons (1–4pm)
Heavy tropical downpour — dramatic but usually only 1-2 hours. Not drizzle — proper rain. Then it clears.
🌅 Evenings (5pm+)
Rain usually stops. Sunsets can be spectacular. Perfect for dinner, sunset cruises, or hot springs.
Does anything close?
🏨 Hotels — All open, covered pools & spa
🎯 Tours — Run rain or shine (guides are used to it)
🏪 Shops — Open as normal
🌿 National Parks — Open as normal
🍽️ Restaurants — All open
♨️ Hot springs — Even BETTER in the rain!

The rhythm: Adventure mornings → chill afternoons (hot springs, chocolate tours, spa, cooking classes) → clear evenings. The rain makes rafting better (higher rivers), wildlife more active (frogs!), and hot springs more atmospheric. Pack a lightweight rain jacket and quick-dry clothes. The upside? Fewer tourists, lower prices, lush green landscapes, and baby turtle nesting season. 🐢
