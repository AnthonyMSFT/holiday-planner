## 🗺️ THE CONCEPT

The Dordogne is where **adventure meets history**. Prehistoric caves with
30,000-year-old paintings, fairy-tale castles perched on cliffs, canoeing down
a river through medieval villages, and some of the best food in France. It's
France's most family-friendly region — everything is designed for exploring
together.

**Stay at a resort campsite** with pools, waterslides, and entertainment as
your base, and make day trips to castles, caves, gorges, and medieval towns.

```
Day 1      Fly MAN → Bordeaux. Pick up car. Drive to Dordogne (2hrs).
           │
Days 1-14  🏰 DORDOGNE (14 nights)
           Resort campsite with pools/waterpark as base
           Day trips: canoe the Dordogne, prehistoric caves, castles,
           cliff villages, markets, adventure parks, cave kayaking
           │
Day 15     Drive to Bordeaux (2hrs). Fly Bordeaux → MAN.
```

## 🎯 DAY TRIPS & ACTIVITIES — MEMORY-MAKERS

### 🛶 CANOEING THE DORDOGNE ⭐⭐⭐ THE #1 ACTIVITY

This is **the** Dordogne experience. Paddle downstream past castles, cliff
villages, and golden limestone cliffs.

- **The classic route:** La Roque-Gageac → Beynac (8km, ~2-3hrs)
- Pass under **Château de Castelnaud** and **Château de Beynac** — two castles facing each other across the river!
- Gentle current — Tom (8) rides with a parent in a 2-person canoe
- Katie & George can share a canoe independently
- Multiple hire companies — Canoës Dordogne, Canoë Loisirs, Canoës Vacances
- ~€15-20pp, they drop you upstream and you paddle back to your car
- **Do this on Day 2 while you're fresh — it sets the tone for the whole holiday**

### 🏰 CASTLES (the Dordogne has more per square mile than almost anywhere)

#### Château de Castelnaud ⭐ #1 FOR KIDS
- Medieval warfare museum — trebuchets, swords, armour, crossbows
- **Daily trebuchet firing demonstrations!** Tom will LOSE HIS MIND.
- Hands-on sword and armour exhibits
- Stunning views over the Dordogne valley
- Entry ~€12 adults, children discounted
- **The best castle for kids in all of France**

#### Château de Beynac ⭐
- Perched dramatically on a cliff 150m above the river
- Filming location for historical movies
- Genuine medieval atmosphere — feels like stepping back in time
- Entry ~€10 adults

#### Château des Milandes
- Former home of Josephine Baker — jazz singer, WWII spy, civil rights icon
- Falconry displays (birds of prey flying overhead!) — **Tom will love this**
- Beautiful gardens
- Great story for Katie — fascinating woman

### 🦴 PREHISTORIC CAVES — 30,000 YEARS OF HISTORY

#### Lascaux IV ⭐⭐⭐ WORLD-FAMOUS
- Replica of the world's most important prehistoric cave paintings
- The original (Lascaux II) is closed; Lascaux IV is a stunning full-scale recreation
- Interactive museum and 3D experiences
- **George will geek out. The paintings are genuinely awe-inspiring.**
- Entry ~€22 adults, children discounted
- Near Montignac, 25 min from Sarlat
- **Book timed entry tickets in advance!** Sells out in August.

#### Gouffre de Proumeyssac ⭐ UNIQUE — "THE CRYSTAL CATHEDRAL"
- Descend into an enormous underground cavern by **glass-bottomed viewing platform**
- Spectacular stalactites, stalagmites, and crystal formations
- Son et lumière (sound and light show) underground
- **Tom will feel like he's in a real-life adventure movie**
- Entry ~€12 adults
- Near Le Bugue, 30 min from Sarlat

#### Grotte de Font-de-Gaume
- One of the last caves in the world where you can see **REAL prehistoric paintings**
- Genuine 17,000-year-old bison, horse, and mammoth paintings
- Very limited daily visitors — **book MONTHS in advance** (or try on the day, arrive early)
- Free! But limited to ~50 visitors/day
- Near Les Eyzies

#### Les Eyzies — "Capital of Prehistory"
- National Museum of Prehistory — excellent, interactive
- Multiple caves and rock shelters in walking distance
- Abri de Cap Blanc — real prehistoric carved sculptures in rock

### 🏔️ CLIFF VILLAGES & VIEWPOINTS

#### La Roque-Gageac ⭐
- Village built into a cliff face right on the Dordogne river
- Tropical garden in the cliff (microclimate!)
- Traditional gabarre (flat-bottomed boat) rides on the river — **Tom loves boats**
- One of "The Most Beautiful Villages in France"

#### Domme ⭐
- Fortified hilltop bastide town with stunning panoramic views over the Dordogne valley
- Underground caves beneath the town square (access from the market hall!)
- Evening visit for sunset views = magical

#### Rocamadour ⭐⭐⭐ JAW-DROPPING
- Medieval pilgrimage town built vertically into a cliff face — 3 levels!
- 216 steps up the Grand Escalier (medieval pilgrims climbed on their knees)
- Sanctuary, castle at the top, village at the bottom
- **One of the most dramatic sights in France**
- 45 min drive from Sarlat, worth it
- Also home to Rocamadour AOC goat cheese — delicious

### ⚡ ADVENTURE ACTIVITIES

#### 🌊 CAVE KAYAKING — GOUFFRE DE PADIRAC ⭐⭐⭐ UNFORGETTABLE
- Descend 75m underground by lift, then **kayak/boat along a subterranean river**
- 1km underground river journey past enormous caverns and stalactites
- One of France's most visited natural sites
- **"We kayaked inside a cave 75 metres underground" = school story gold**
- Entry ~€16 adults, children discounted
- Near Rocamadour, 1hr from Sarlat
- **Book timed entry online — huge queues in August without it**

#### 🧗 ACCROBRANCHE / TREETOP ADVENTURE
- Multiple treetop adventure parks in the Dordogne
- **La Forêt des Écureuils** (near Sarlat) — zip lines, rope bridges, through oak forest
- Courses for all ages from age 4 to extreme adult
- ~€15-25pp
- Katie & George: black course. Tom: his own junior adventure.

#### 🏊 RIVER SWIMMING
- The Dordogne river has multiple safe swimming spots
- Warm in August (22-25°C)
- Free! Just find a beach (many along the river between Sarlat and Beynac)
- Combine with a picnic — very French!

#### 🚴 CYCLING THE VOIE VERTE
- Traffic-free cycle path along the old railway line
- Flat, safe, shaded — perfect for Tom
- Bike hire at multiple points
- Ride through villages, stop for ice cream

#### 🦅 PARC LE BOURNAT — OPEN-AIR LIVING MUSEUM ⭐ ALL AGES
- Recreated 1900s Périgord village — working farm, traditional crafts
- Fairground rides (vintage carousel, swing boats)
- Bread-making, candle-making, traditional games
- Tom can feed animals and ride ponies
- Teens may roll their eyes then secretly enjoy it
- Near Le Bugue, 30 min from Sarlat

#### 🦕 PRÉHISTO PARC
- Walk through a forest with life-size Neanderthal and Cro-Magnon scenes
- Interactive prehistoric experience
- Perfect for Tom after seeing Lascaux
- Near Les Eyzies

#### 🏊 AQUA PARK — AQUA VÉZÈRE (Le Bugue)
- Outdoor water park with slides, pools, wave features
- Good for a half day when you want easy fun
- ~€10-15pp

## 🍽️ FOOD — THE DORDOGNE IS FRANCE'S FOOD CAPITAL

The Périgord (Dordogne's old name) is arguably where France eats best:

| Food | What | Where | Tom-friendly? |
|------|------|-------|--------------|
| **Confit de canard** | Duck leg slow-cooked in its own fat. Crispy, melting. | Every restaurant | ✅ |
| **Pommes sarladaises** | Potatoes fried in duck fat with garlic + parsley | Sarlat restaurants | ✅ Kids love these |
| **Foie gras** | Duck liver pâté — the Dordogne's most famous product | Markets, restaurants | Maybe for teens! |
| **Walnut cake / tarte aux noix** | Rich walnut tart | Bakeries everywhere | ✅ |
| **Crêpes & galettes** | Sweet + savoury pancakes | Market stalls, crêperies | ✅ |
| **Rocamadour cheese** | Tiny, pungent goat cheese rounds | Markets (buy at Rocamadour!) | Maybe! |
| **Strawberries** | Périgord strawberries are legendary (in season!) | Markets | ✅ |
| **Truffle products** | Truffle oil, truffle crisps, truffle omelettes | Sarlat market, specialist shops | Truffle crisps = ✅ |
| **French bread + cheese** | Baguette + Comté + saucisson = perfect picnic | Any supermarket/bakery | ✅ |

**Sarlat Saturday market** is one of the best in France — arrive early, taste everything.

**Self-catering tip:** French supermarkets (Carrefour, Intermarché, Super U) near Sarlat are excellent. Markets even better.

## 🗒️ PRACTICAL NOTES

- **No direct LBA flights.** MAN → Bordeaux (easyJet/Ryanair direct, 2hrs) is best. Or MAN → Toulouse (Ryanair, 2hrs) — same drive to Dordogne.
- **Bordeaux → Sarlat drive:** 2hrs via A89 then D roads. Scenic once you leave the motorway.
- **Sarlat-la-Canéda:** The "capital" of the Dordogne. Golden medieval old town, fantastic market (Wed & Sat). Base yourself within 20 min of here.
- **August heat:** 30-35°C typical. Canoe trips, river swimming, and caves are perfect for hot days (caves are 13°C underground — bring a jumper!).
- **Lascaux IV booking:** This WILL sell out on your dates. Book as early as possible.
- **Gouffre de Padirac:** Arrive by 9am without a booking, or book online. Underground is 13°C — jackets essential.
- **Canoeing:** No experience needed. Gentle current does the work. Life jackets provided. Companies drop you upstream and you paddle back. Easy and safe.
- **15 August (Ferragosto):** Big French holiday. Many things closed. Roads very busy. Plan accordingly for your departure day.
- **Currency:** Euro. Tap water safe. Card widely accepted but take some cash for markets.
- **Language:** French. Less English spoken than coastal tourist areas — learn basics, locals will warm to you.
