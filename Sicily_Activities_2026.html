🇮🇹 The Holiday at a Glance

Two weeks on the volcanic island where ancient Greek ruins meet turquoise coves and the best street food in the Mediterranean. Base yourself in Taormina for week one — Mount Etna guided tours, the Alcantara Gorge for body rafting, and the beautiful Isola Bella beach. Week two moves to the quieter southeast: Syracuse's 2,700-year-old Greek theatre, the baroque masterpiece of Noto, hidden beaches like Calamosche, and the charming fishing village of Marzamemi.

Tom will be wide-eyed on the cable car and 4x4 ride up Etna to nearly 3,000 metres. George will love the body rafting through a volcanic gorge. Katie will photograph every golden-stone baroque building in Noto. And everyone will eat their body weight in arancini, granita, and cannoli. Sicily is Italy turned up to eleven — bigger flavours, bigger landscapes, bigger adventures.

🔥 Scorching, dry, and very sunny. Sicily in August is seriously hot — inland areas can hit 40°C with sirocco winds from Africa. Coastal areas are more bearable with sea breezes. Rain is almost non-existent. The sea is bath-warm at 27-28°C. Schedule Mt Etna tours for early morning. Carry water everywhere and seek shade midday.
