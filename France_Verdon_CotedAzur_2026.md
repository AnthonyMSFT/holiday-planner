## 🏔️ WEEK 1: GORGES DU VERDON (7 nights)

### What are the Gorges du Verdon?
- **Europe's Grand Canyon** — up to 700m deep, 25km long
- Stunning turquoise river and lake (Lac de Sainte-Croix)
- Surrounded by lavender fields, hilltop villages, and Provençal countryside
- World-class adventure activities: kayaking, canyoning, rafting, climbing
- Heart of Provence — markets, rosé wine, cicadas

### Where to stay:

#### OPTION A: Yelloh! Village Domaine du Verdon ⭐ RECOMMENDED
- **Location:** Castellane, at the eastern gateway to the gorge
- **Website:** yellohvillage.co.uk
- **Facilities:** Pool complex with waterslides, kids pool, heated pools, restaurant, bar, mini club, teen activities, mini golf, multisport court, evening entertainment
- **Mobile homes:** 2-3 bed, AC, terrace, up to 6 people
- **Why:** Purpose-built family resort + right at the gorge entrance
- **Price estimate:** **£1,000-1,800 for 7 nights**

#### OPTION B: Camping Verdon Provence (Quinson)
- Western end of the gorge, near Lac de Quinson
- Pool, restaurant, quieter, direct gorge access
- **Price estimate:** **£600-1,000 for 7 nights**

#### OPTION C: Eurocamp parks nearby
- Eurocamp have several parks in Provence/Var — check eurocamp.co.uk
- Search "Provence" or "Côte d'Azur" region
- **Lei Suves** (Roquebrune-sur-Argens) is popular but closer to the coast

### 🎯 Things to do — Week 1:

#### 🛶 KAYAKING ON LAC DE SAINTE-CROIX ⭐⭐⭐ HIGHLIGHT
- Paddle into the mouth of the Gorges du Verdon on turquoise water
- Hire kayaks or pedal boats from the beach at Sainte-Croix-du-Verdon
- The water is an unreal shade of turquoise — like the Caribbean
- **Tom-friendly** — pedal boats available for the whole family
- Katie & George can take a 2-person kayak deeper into the gorge
- ~€15-25/hour for kayak hire
- **The "wow" day of the holiday**

#### 🌊 SWIMMING IN THE GORGE
- Multiple swimming spots along the Verdon river
- Pont du Galetas — where the river meets the lake, easy access
- Water is clean but cool — refreshing in August heat!
- Natural rock "beaches" for sunbathing

#### ⚡ CANYONING (Katie & George) ⭐
- Guided canyon adventures in the Verdon tributaries
- Abseiling, jumping into pools, sliding down natural water chutes
- Min age usually 10-12 — **perfect for Katie & George**
- Various levels from easy to extreme
- Half-day tours ~€40-60pp
- Operators: Aboard Rafting, Verdon Passion, Feel Rafting

#### ⚡ WHITE WATER RAFTING
- Rafting on the Verdon river, Class II-III rapids
- Family-friendly options available (Tom can join with parents)
- Half-day ~€35-50pp
- Thrilling but safe with experienced guides

#### 🌸 LAVENDER FIELDS (late July = peak bloom!)
- You're visiting at PERFECT lavender timing
- Plateau de Valensole — endless purple fields, 30 min drive
- Stop for photos, buy lavender products
- **Instagram heaven for Katie**
- Combine with a Provençal market visit

#### 🏛️ MOUSTIERS-SAINTE-MARIE ⭐
- One of the "Most Beautiful Villages in France"
- Perched on a clifftop with a gold star suspended between two cliffs
- Famous for faience (painted pottery)
- Stunning waterfall through the village
- Great for a half-day visit + lunch

#### 🏛️ PROVENÇAL MARKETS
- Castellane market (Wed & Sat)
- Riez market (Wed & Sat) — lavender capital
- Aups truffle market (Wed & Sat)
- Olives, cheese, saucisson, honey, lavender — taste everything!

#### 🏔️ ROUTE DES CRÊTES (Scenic drive) ⭐
- Spectacular cliff-edge road along the north rim of the gorge
- Multiple viewpoints looking 700m down into the canyon
- Point Sublime — the most famous viewpoint
- Balcons de la Mescla — where two gorges meet
- **Drive it as a half-day excursion — stop at every viewpoint**
- Free (it's a public road)

#### 🏊 LAC DE SAINTE-CROIX
- Large turquoise lake at the western end of the gorge
- Sandy/pebbly beaches for swimming and sunbathing
- Water sports: paddleboards, pedalos, small sailing boats
- Multiple beach areas — Bauduen and Sainte-Croix villages
- Tom can swim and play in shallow areas while teens kayak

#### 🪂 BUNGEE JUMPING — PONT DE L'ARTUBY ⭐ ONCE-IN-A-LIFETIME (Katie/George)
- **Europe's highest bungee jump** from a bridge — 182m above the gorge floor!
- Located on the Route des Crêtes
- Min age 16 — **Katie (17) qualifies!** George (15) may need to check.
- ~€130pp — expensive but genuinely life-changing
- Even watching from the bridge is incredible
- Operator: Latitude Challenge
- **"I bungee jumped off Europe's highest bridge" = ultimate school story**

#### 🧗 VIA FERRATA — GORGES DU VERDON
- Iron-runged climbing routes bolted into the gorge cliffs
- Several routes near Castellane, varying difficulty
- Safe (you're clipped to a steel cable the whole time) but thrilling
- Min age usually 10-12 with parent — **Katie, George, and possibly Tom**
- ~€35-50pp with guide and equipment
- You're literally climbing the walls of Europe's Grand Canyon

#### 🌟 STARGAZING IN THE VERDON
- Almost zero light pollution in the Verdon area
- August = peak Perseid meteor shower (10-13 Aug!)
- Lie on the ground after dinner and watch shooting stars
- **Free, unforgettable, and you're there at exactly the right time**
- Plateau de Valensole or any viewpoint away from the campsite
- Tom will count the shooting stars. Katie & George will never forget it.

## 🏖️ WEEK 2: CÔTE D'AZUR — MEMORY-MAKING EDITION (7 nights)

### Getting there:
Drive Verdon → Fréjus coast: ~1.5hrs via scenic roads through Provence.

### Where to stay:

#### OPTION A: Camping Lei Suves (Roquebrune-sur-Argens) ⭐ EUROCAMP AVAILABLE
- **Location:** 10km inland from Fréjus/Saint-Raphaël beaches
- **Website:** eurocamp.co.uk (search "Lei Suves")
- **Facilities:** Large pool complex with slides and splash area, restaurant, bar, kids club, evening entertainment, mini golf, sports courts
- **Bookable via Eurocamp** — courier service, linen packs included
- **Price estimate:** **£1,200-2,200 for 7 nights** (via Eurocamp)

#### OPTION B: Yelloh! Village Les Tournels (Ramatuelle/Saint-Tropez)
- Near Saint-Tropez! Stunning hilltop location with sea views
- Pool complex, waterslides, spa, restaurant
- Direct shuttle to Pampelonne Beach
- More upmarket, pricier
- **Price estimate:** **£1,500-2,800 for 7 nights**

#### OPTION C: Camping de la Baume / Camping Douce Quiétude (Fréjus)
- Waterpark-style pool complexes
- Close to Fréjus beaches and Aqualand waterpark
- Good value
- **Price estimate:** **£800-1,400 for 7 nights**

### 🎯 Things to do — Week 2:

#### 🏖️ BEACHES
- **Fréjus Plage** — long sandy beach, all facilities, shallow water (Tom-friendly)
- **Saint-Raphaël** — sandy, promenade, restaurants, water sports
- **Pampelonne Beach** (Saint-Tropez) — famous 5km sandy beach, beach clubs
- **Calanques de l'Estérel** ⭐ — stunning red rock coves with turquoise water. Like a mini Sardinia. Drive the coast road and stop at secret coves.
- **Plage de la Nartelle** (Sainte-Maxime) — sandy, family-friendly

#### 🌊 AQUALAND FRÉJUS ⭐ AQUA PARK!
- One of France's biggest water parks
- Multiple huge slides, wave pool, lazy river, kids zones
- Perfect for a full day — Tom will be in heaven, teens love the big slides
- Entry ~€30-35pp (children discounted)
- **This ticks your aqua park box!**

#### 🏛️ NICE (1hr drive) ⭐
- Promenade des Anglais — iconic seafront walk
- Old Town (Vieux Nice) — narrow streets, markets, socca (chickpea pancake)
- Castle Hill — climb or take lift for panoramic views
- Cours Saleya market — flowers, food, antiques
- Beach (pebble) — swimming + people watching
- Great for a full day trip

#### 🏛️ MONACO / MONTE CARLO (1.5hr drive) ⭐ TEENS WILL LOVE
- See the Casino (can't enter under 18, but the square is stunning)
- Prince's Palace — changing of the guard at 11:55am
- Oceanographic Museum — incredible aquarium built into the cliff
- F1 circuit — walk the actual Grand Prix track through the streets!
- **George will go mad for the cars and F1 circuit**
- Exotic Garden — cacti garden with cave
- Tiny country — you can walk the whole thing!

#### 🚤 SAINT-TROPEZ (40 min drive)
- Take the boat from Sainte-Maxime across the bay (more fun than driving!)
- Wander the harbour — superyachts, cafés, boutiques
- Place des Lices — famous boules square, market (Tue & Sat)
- Pampelonne Beach
- Katie will love the glamour; not much for Tom beyond the boat ride

#### 🏛️ CANNES (45 min drive)
- La Croisette — the famous palm-lined promenade
- Palais des Festivals — walk the red carpet steps (film festival venue!)
- Île Sainte-Marguerite — ferry to island, fort where the Man in the Iron Mask was held
- Beaches (sandy, but many are paid/private in Cannes)

#### 🏔️ CALANQUES DE L'ESTÉREL ⭐⭐⭐ HIDDEN GEM
- Dramatic red porphyry rock formations meeting turquoise sea
- Short walks from car parks lead to hidden coves
- Calanque du Petit Caneiret, Calanque de Maubois — stunning swimming
- **Free, less crowded than beaches, incredibly beautiful**
- Pack snorkelling gear — the water is crystal clear
- Best from Saint-Raphaël coast road

#### 🤿 SNORKELLING TRAIL — SENTIER SOUS-MARIN (Saint-Raphaël) ⭐ UNIQUE
- **France's only marked underwater snorkelling trail!**
- At Plage du Pourrousset, Boulouris (near Saint-Raphaël)
- Free! Just bring your own mask and snorkel (or rent for ~€5)
- Underwater info boards identify fish and sea life as you swim along
- Protected marine area — expect to see wrasse, sea bream, octopus, sea urchins
- **Tom will feel like Jacques Cousteau. Katie & George will love the fish spotting.**
- Shallow enough for Tom (1-5m depth)

#### 🚤 HIRE A BOAT — CAPTAIN YOUR OWN (no licence needed!) ⭐⭐⭐
- In France you can hire small motorboats **without a licence** (up to 6hp)
- Rent from Fréjus, Saint-Raphaël, or Sainte-Maxime harbour
- Putter along the Estérel coast, find your own secret cove, anchor and swim
- ~€80-150 for half day for the whole family
- **Being your own captain = the ultimate family memory**
- Pack a picnic, stop in a cove, swim off the back of the boat
- Bigger boats (with licence or skipper) also available

#### 🏎️ KARTING — CIRCUIT DE PUGET-SUR-ARGENS ⭐ GEORGE WILL LOVE THIS
- Outdoor go-kart circuit near Fréjus
- Multiple kart categories including junior karts for Tom (8+)
- Adult/teen karts hit decent speeds
- ~€15-25 per session
- **Great after the Monaco F1 day — "now it's YOUR turn"**

#### 🎆 FIREWORKS ON THE BEACH
- French Riviera towns have **fireworks displays throughout August**
- Fréjus, Saint-Raphaël, Cannes, and Nice all have summer firework nights
- Check local listings — usually free, watch from the beach
- **Fireworks over the Mediterranean = magical family moment**

#### 🧗 AVENTURE PARC — TREETOP ADVENTURE (Roquebrune-sur-Argens)
- High-ropes treetop course near Lei Suves campsite
- Multiple difficulty levels — courses for Tom (8+) through to adult black courses
- Zip lines through the pine forest
- ~€15-25pp
- Katie & George get the challenging routes, Tom gets his own adventure

#### 🍦 NICE OLD TOWN FOOD TOUR — DIY FAMILY VERSION ⭐
- Create your own food trail through Vieux Nice:
  1. **Socca** at Chez Theresa (chickpea pancake, hot from the oven) — ~€3
  2. **Pissaladière** from a bakery on Cours Saleya — ~€3
  3. **Ice cream** at Fenocchio (96 flavours! Lavender, black olive, tomato-basil...) — ~€3
  4. **Pan bagnat** (Nice's famous tuna sandwich) — ~€5
  5. **Tarte tropézienne** from the pastry shop — ~€4
- Total family food tour: ~€40 and everyone is stuffed
- **Eating weird ice cream flavours = a memory in itself**

#### 🌅 SUNSET BOAT TRIP — ESTÉREL BY SEA ⭐⭐⭐ UNFORGETTABLE
- Evening boat trips along the red Estérel coast at sunset
- The red rocks turn deep crimson as the sun drops — stunning
- Some operators include aperitif and swimming stop
- ~€30-40pp
- Depart from Saint-Raphaël
- **The adults get their moment too — rosé, sunset, Mediterranean**

#### 🏊 CLIFF JUMPING — CALANQUES SECRET

- Low jumps (2-3m) for Tom-sized courage, higher for teens
- Ask locals or lifeguards for the safe spots
- **Free, thrilling, and the photos will be incredible**

## 🗒️ PRACTICAL NOTES

- **Currency:** Euro
- **Driving:** Right-hand side. Autoroutes are tolled (pay at booths). Verdon gorge roads are winding but well-maintained.
- **Route des Crêtes:** Narrow cliff-edge road. Not for nervous drivers! Spectacular but take it slow. Some sections one-way.
- **Lavender timing:** Peak bloom is typically mid-July to early August. By 12 Aug some fields may be harvested — visit in Week 1 to be safe!
- **August in the south of France:** Hot! 30-38°C. Do sightseeing mornings/evenings. Beach/pool/gorge swimming afternoon.
- **Nice beaches:** Pebble, not sand. Bring water shoes. Fréjus/Saint-Raphaël beaches are sandy.
- **Monaco:** Tiny! You can walk everywhere. Dress code at Casino square area is smart-casual.
- **Saint-Tropez by boat:** Much more fun (and easier parking!) than driving. Bateaux Verts run from Sainte-Maxime, ~€8pp each way.
- **Lac de Sainte-Croix:** Arrive early in August — car parks fill up by 10-11am.
- **Language:** French, but English widely spoken in tourist areas on the coast. Less so inland in Verdon — learn basic French, locals appreciate it.
- **Eurocamp at Lei Suves:** Gives you courier service, welcome pack, linen. Park has its own pool complex. Good base for coast exploration.
