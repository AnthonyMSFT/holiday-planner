🇨🇷 The Holiday at a Glance

Forget hotel-hopping — this is the single-base version. Plant yourselves at an eco-lodge with volcanic hot springs and a pool, and let Arenal / La Fortuna be your launchpad for 14 days of pure adventure. Within 30 minutes you’ve got a volcano to hike, a 70-metre waterfall to swim under, white-water rapids to raft, cloud-forest canopy to zip-line through, and more sloths than you can count. Day trips to Río Celeste (the bluest waterfall on Earth), Monteverde’s cloud forest, and Caño Negro’s wildlife wetlands mean you never run out of things to do.

George will want to raft every river and rappel every waterfall. Tom will be obsessed with the sloths at Proyecto Asis and the frogs at the Ranario. Katie will photograph the turquoise Río Celeste and the hot springs at sunset. And on your rest days? Soak in volcanic hot springs with a cocktail while the kids bomb the water slides. This is adventure-on-tap with zero packing stress.

🌧️ Rainy Season — What It's Actually Like

August is "green season" — but don't panic. Here's the reality:

☀️ Mornings (7am–1pm)
Almost always sunny and dry. This is when you do all outdoor activities — hikes, zip lines, rafting, beach, waterfall trips.
🌧️ Afternoons (1–4pm)
Heavy tropical downpour — dramatic but usually only 1-2 hours. Not drizzle — proper rain. Then it clears.
🌅 Evenings (5pm+)
Rain usually stops. Sunsets can be spectacular. Perfect for dinner, sunset cruises, or hot springs.
Does anything close?
🏨 Hotels — All open, covered pools & spa
🎯 Tours — Run rain or shine (guides are used to it)
🏪 Shops — Open as normal
🌿 National Parks — Open as normal
🍽️ Restaurants — All open
♨️ Hot springs — Even BETTER in the rain!

The rhythm: Adventure mornings → chill afternoons (hot springs, chocolate tours, spa, cooking classes) → clear evenings. The rain makes rafting better (higher rivers), wildlife more active (frogs!), and hot springs more atmospheric. Pack a lightweight rain jacket and quick-dry clothes. The upside? Fewer tourists, lower prices, lush green landscapes, and baby turtle nesting season. 🐢
