🇿🇲 The Holiday at a Glance

This isn’t a beach holiday — this is the adventure of a lifetime. Stand on the edge of the world’s largest waterfall. Take a helicopter flight over the thundering Zambezi. Raft Grade 5 white water through Batoka Gorge. Then fly into the African bush for game drives where you’ll see elephants, lions, leopards, and hippos from an open-top Land Cruiser. Zambia is raw, real, and utterly unforgettable — and August is the perfect time to go (dry season = best wildlife + Devil’s Pool is open).

George will be counting down to the bungee jump off Victoria Falls Bridge — 111 metres above the Zambezi. Tom will be speechless watching elephants drink at sunset from your lodge deck. Katie will photograph the rainbow mist over the Falls and the Milky Way from a bush camp with zero light pollution. And the whole family will never forget a canoe safari past hippos, or a night drive spotting leopards by spotlight. This is a “once in a lifetime” that’s actually achievable.

☀️ Peak dry season — the best time to visit. Zero rain, warm sunny days (25-28°C), and cool mornings/evenings (pack layers for dawn game drives!). Wildlife congregates around water sources making spotting easy. Victoria Falls has lower water = clearer views + Devil’s Pool is open + rafting season. Mosquitoes are minimal but still take malaria prophylaxis. Stunning clear skies for stargazing from the bush.
